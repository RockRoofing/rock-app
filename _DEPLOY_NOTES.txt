DEPLOY NOTES - "Average Time to Get Paid" changed to "Average Days Beyond Terms"
================================================================================

NEW DEFINITION (as requested)
-----------------------------
Per fully-paid sales invoice:
    daysBeyondTerms = Paid Date (FullyPaidOnDate) - Due Date
      positive = paid LATE, negative = paid EARLY, 0 = on terms.
The metric = the AVERAGE of daysBeyondTerms across all qualifying paid invoices.
Example: 10 invoices each paid 5 days after their due date -> average = +5.

Changes from before:
  - Measured against the DUE DATE, not the invoice date.
  - Early payments (negatives) now COUNT (previously the days<0 filter dropped
    them, which would have skewed the average).
  - The headline is a true invoice-weighted average (not an average of monthly
    averages), so it matches your example exactly.
  - Sanity window widened to -365..+365 days so early payments are kept.

CARD BEHAVIOUR
--------------
  - Label: "Average Days Beyond Terms".
  - Value shows "+N days late", "N days early", or "On terms".
  - RAG dot uses lower-is-better: at/below target = green, up to 15 days over =
    amber, beyond = red. Target defaults to 0 (paid on time); editable.
  - Trend line plots each month's average (can go negative).
  - Drill ("Days Beyond Terms - All Paid Invoices") now shows Due Date, Paid Date,
    and a signed "Days +/- Terms" column per invoice.

DATA USED (all already on the stored invoice lines)
---------------------------------------------------
dueDate, fullyPaidOnDate, status, amountDue, amountPaid. An invoice counts as paid
if status is 'PAID' OR nothing is outstanding (amountDue == 0 and amountPaid > 0).
Credit notes excluded. Scans ALL projects (paid invoices often sit on completed
jobs). If fullyPaidOnDate is missing on older lines, a re-sync fills it (the on-card
amber diagnostic will say so, and now also reports how many have a due date).
