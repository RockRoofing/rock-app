DEPLOY NOTES - Gross Margin now driven by the Account Categorisation tab
========================================================================

WHY IT WAS WRONG
----------------
The Gross Margin was using Xero's OWN P&L section grouping (its "Income" and
"Cost of Sales" section totals) to decide what counts as sales vs cost. That does
NOT match your Account Categorisation tab. Any account Xero files under Overheads
(or anywhere other than Cost of Sales) was dropped from costs even if you had
tagged it Materials/Labour - so costs came out too low and the margin too high.

THE FIX (your instruction: use the Account Categorisation tab as the arbiter)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Gross Margin is now computed by bucketing EVERY P&L account code by its category in
the Account Categorisation tab (config:account-categorisation), using the exact same
category logic as that page (saved category wins; legacy 'ignore' -> 'overheads';
otherwise the per-code default):

    Total Sales  = sum of codes categorised 'sales'
    Total Costs  = sum of codes categorised 'labour' + 'materials'
    (overheads and uncategorised are EXCLUDED)
    Gross Profit = Total Sales - Total Costs
    Gross Margin = Gross Profit / Total Sales

So the formula is: (Total Sales - (Total Labour + Total Materials)) / Total Sales.

Whatever you tag in the Account Categorisation tab now directly drives the number.
Re-tag an account there and the Gross Margin updates on the next Scorecard load.

Credit notes: still handled automatically. Xero's P&L is accrual-based and already
nets sales credit notes into the revenue codes and supplier credit notes into the
cost codes, so no separate /CreditNotes fetch (that would double count).

Still: last 6 completed months, rolling, live-fetched from Xero each load. Headline
= latest completed month. The card drill now shows, per month: Sales, Labour,
Materials, Total Costs, Gross Profit, Gross Margin.

FILES (both EDITS - no new files this time; xero-gross-margin.js already exists)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - pages/api/xero-gross-margin.js       (rewritten to use categorisation)
  - pages/commercial-scorecard.js        (drill now shows Labour/Materials split)

HOW TO DEPLOY (GitHub web editor)
---------------------------------
Replace both files at their paths. Commit -> Vercel auto-deploys.

PREREQUISITE - Chart of Accounts must be synced
-----------------------------------------------
The categorisation-based split needs each P&L line to carry its account CODE. That
mapping comes from the Chart of Accounts. If you have already synced the Chart of
Accounts (Account Categorisation page shows your codes), you are fine. If some P&L
lines have no code mapping they simply won't be bucketed - so make sure the Chart
of Accounts is synced and every real P&L account is categorised (not left
'uncategorised', which is excluded from costs by design).

VERIFY
------
  - Open the Commercial Scorecard -> Gross Margin card. Click it.
  - The table lists 6 months: Sales / Labour / Materials / Total Costs / Gross
    Profit / Gross Margin.
  - Cross-check one month: Total Costs should equal the sum of every account you
    tagged Labour or Materials in the Account Categorisation tab for that month;
    Sales should equal your Sales-tagged accounts (net of credit notes).
  - If a figure looks off, it's almost always a mis-tagged account in the
    Account Categorisation tab - fix it there and reload.
