THE BUG (your screenshot)
-------------------------
Cost of Sale showed Xero -£967 vs App £145,943 — clearly wrong. Cause: the P&L
benchmark flattened the ENTIRE Profit & Loss into a single {accountName: amount}
map with no section context. The page then treated "anything not sales/wages" as
Bills — so overheads, admin expenses and P&L SUBTOTAL rows all polluted the Bills
figure (and netted it to near-zero / negative).

THE FIX
-------
• fetchProfitAndLoss now records each account's P&L SECTION and returns section
  totals: incomeTotal + costOfSalesTotal + a bySection breakdown.
• The benchmark now stores this structure per month.
• The Bookkeeping summary now uses ONLY:
    - Sales   = the Income section total
    - Costs   = the Cost of Sales section only (overheads/admin EXCLUDED),
                split into Direct Wages vs Bills by account name.
  So the three rows now reflect true cost-of-sale, not the whole P&L.
• Row labels clarified to match intent:
    "Total in Xero"  |  "Categorised in app"  |  "Still to find"
  Green when they balance, red when there's still an amount to categorise.
• Back-compat: if an OLD flat benchmark is still cached, the page falls back to
  name-based heuristics until you re-sync. Press Sync Xero figures to get the
  accurate section-based numbers.

NOTE ON NAMING: the CoS wages split keys on account names containing
wage/PAYE/salaries/"CIS labour". If your Xero Cost of Sales account names differ,
tell me the exact names and I'll match them precisely.
