WHAT THIS IS
------------
The Cash Flow tab is now a proper 13-week rolling weekly forecast that pulls everything
together:

MONEY IN
  - Invoices owed  -> landed on the EXPECTED date from Invoices Owed (falls back to due
    date if no expected date set).
  - Retention releases -> landed on each unreceived release date from the Retention
    Tracker.
  - VAT refunds -> landed at month-end, using your filed Box 5 (or the estimate) from the
    VAT Refund page.

MONEY OUT
  - Bills to pay -> on their due date (from the Bills sync).
  - Overheads -> timed by the CASH SCHEDULE you set up, using the Budgets amounts
    (spread evenly / one day / specific days, with carry-forwards applied).
  - VAT payments -> month-end, when Box 5 is a payment.

Opening balance = cash at bank from your Xero bank summary (editable to model scenarios).
Running weekly closing balance, with the lowest point highlighted and negative weeks shown
in red. Two charts (balance line; weekly in vs out) and a full week-by-week table.

DEPENDENCIES (for accurate numbers)
-----------------------------------
It reads the STORED data from the other pages, so keep them synced:
  - Bills to Pay (sync)          -> bills out
  - Invoices Owed (sync)         -> invoices in + expected dates
  - VAT Refund                    -> filed Box 5 / estimate  (click Refresh estimate once)
  - Cash Schedule + Budgets       -> overheads out
  - Bank summary (Cash Flow syncs it) -> opening balance
If a source is empty, that line simply shows as nothing - no crash.

NOTE / LIKELY FIRST-LOOK ITEMS
------------------------------
- Retention only lands if the release has a DATE set in the Retention Tracker (undated
  releases can't be timed - they show on Retentions Due as "TBC").
- Overheads only appear for lines you gave a timing in Cash Schedule.
- Sales pipeline is intentionally NOT included yet.
