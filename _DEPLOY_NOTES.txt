DEPLOY NOTES - Gross Margin 6.5% is wrong (expected 22-27%): diagnostic build
=============================================================================

FIRST, TO ANSWER YOUR QUESTION
------------------------------
No - the Gross Margin is NOT a rolling/cumulative 6-month figure. Each month is
calculated on its OWN month's P&L only, and the headline shows the LATEST single
month. The trend line plots the 6 individual months. So 6.5% is one month's number,
not a 6-month blend. (If you WANT it to be a rolling 6-month figure - total sales
and total costs summed across 6 months, one margin - say the word and I'll switch
it. That is a real option and might be what you're expecting.)

WHY 6.5% IS ALMOST CERTAINLY WRONG
----------------------------------
A margin that low vs an expected 22-27% means either Sales is being UNDER-counted or
Costs are being OVER-counted in the categorisation bucketing. The likeliest cause is
the P&L-name-to-code mapping: the Gross Margin calc only sees an account if its P&L
name maps to a code in the Chart of Accounts. If a big Sales account doesn't map,
sales is understated and the margin collapses. I can't tell which from here without
seeing the actual numbers - so this build EXPOSES them.

WHAT THIS BUILD ADDS (diagnostic - no formula change yet)
---------------------------------------------------------
Click the Gross Margin card. Under the 6-month table there is now a
"Per-code breakdown - <latest month> (diagnostic)" section showing, for every P&L
code that month:

     -> overheads & uncategorised are shown amber (they're EXCLUDED from the calc)

   - the amount
Plus a line with Xero's own raw section totals (Income, Cost of Sales, Overheads)
