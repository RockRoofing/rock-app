Deploy on top of batch 1.

WHAT CHANGED
------------
The Retention Tracker now shows four new columns for Xero-synced projects, pulled
automatically from Xero (no manual entry):

  • Invoiced  — total invoiced EX-VAT (reconciles against the Final Account, which
                is also ex-VAT), so you can check the retention figure is right.
  • VAT       — total VAT charged across the project's invoices (sits OUTSIDE the
                Final Account).
  • Paid      — total received from the customer (inc VAT), from Xero AmountPaid.
  • Final Bal — Final Account minus amount paid (ex-VAT). Reaches £0 (shown green)
                once the whole final account is cleared; positive (blue) while
                money is still outstanding. VAT is handled correctly: paid ex-VAT
                = paid inc-VAT minus the VAT charged, so VAT doesn't distort the
                balance.

HOW IT WORKS
------------
• The daily Xero sync (wip-sync cron) now also captures, per project: invoiced
  ex-VAT (Σ SubTotal), VAT (Σ TotalTax) and paid (Σ AmountPaid), cached alongside
  the existing invoice totals. Per-invoice subTotal/totalTax are cached too (for
  the upcoming invoice list).
• The dashboard API exposes invoicedExVat / vat / paid per project.
• The Retention Tracker reads them for each Xero project row.

IMPORTANT — first-run timing
----------------------------
The new figures populate after the NEXT daily Xero sync runs (the cache is
rebuilt with the ex-VAT / VAT / paid breakdown). Until then, Invoiced/VAT/Paid/
Final Bal show "—" for existing rows. You can trigger the sync manually if you
have the cron endpoint to hand, otherwise they appear after the next run.
Manual (non-Xero) rows show "—" for these columns — the manual/existing-project
auto-fill comes in batch 3.
