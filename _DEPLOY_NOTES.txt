THE PROBLEM
-----------
The WIP total differed between the WIP page, the EOM/Project Financials table, and the
WIP tab on each project's detail page — because each computed WIP with its own copy of
the formula, and they had drifted:
  • The project WIP tab capped costs at the CURRENT month-end, not the SELECTED
    month's end (so past months pulled in too many costs), and it grossed costs +
    adjustments together at one margin instead of each adjustment at its own margin.
  • Small differences in margin handling between the three.

THE FIX
-------
New lib/wipCalc.js holds ONE authoritative WIP calculation. The WIP page API, the
EOM/Project Financials table (commercial.js) and the project WIP tab
(project/[id].js) now ALL call it, so the number is identical everywhere. It uses:
  • Costs after the valuation date to the END OF THE SELECTED MONTH (empty if the
    valuation date is the last day of the month).
  • Live achieved margin ((invoiced − costs to val date) / invoiced), project override
    wins.
  • Each manual adjustment grossed at its own margin (default = project margin);
    positive and negative amounts handled symmetrically.

The WIP page remains the source of truth; the other two now mirror its logic exactly.
