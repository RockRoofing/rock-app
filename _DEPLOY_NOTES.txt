WHY THE APP AND XERO DIDN'T MATCH (your question)
-------------------------------------------------
The grey "P&L (Xero)" figure under the COST OF SALE (BILLS) card was showing the
WHOLE Cost of Sales (£117,011) — which INCLUDES Direct Wages (£10,445) and every
cost-of-sale line regardless of your categorisation. That's why it didn't line up
with the Bills card (which excludes wages and only counts Materials/Labour codes).

THE RULE WE'RE NOW APPLYING (as you specified)
----------------------------------------------
• Cost of Sale (Bills) grey P&L = sum of P&L cost lines whose ACCOUNT CODE is
  marked Materials or Labour in the app's Account Categorisation, EXCLUDING
  Direct Wages (320).
• Direct Wages grey P&L = P&L code 320 only.
• Sales grey P&L = P&L code 200. (unchanged — was already correct)

WHAT HAD TO CHANGE (the P&L benchmark didn't store account codes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The P&L benchmark only stored account NAMES, so it couldn't be filtered by your
(code-based) categorisation. Now:
• lib/xero.js: new fetchAccountCodeMap() (Chart of Accounts -> code); fetchProfit
  AndLoss() now also returns byCode { code: amount }.
• deep-sync.js + sync-benchmark.js: fetch the code map once and store byCode in
  xero:pl-benchmark.
• bookkeeping.js (front-end): grey P&L per card computed from byCode filtered by
  config:account-categorisation (materials/labour), 320 excluded from Bills,
  320-only for Wages, 200 for Sales.

*** ACTION REQUIRED AFTER DEPLOY ***
Click "↻ Sync Xero figures" on the Bookkeeping page ONCE. This re-pulls the P&L
WITH account codes (byCode) so the grey references recompute to the new rule.
Until that re-sync runs, the grey Bills figure falls back to the old section total.

NOTE ON THE REMAINING SMALL GAP
-------------------------------
After this, the grey Bills P&L reference will only include codes you've marked
Materials/Labour. If Fuel/Hotels/Design etc. are NOT marked Materials/Labour in
Account Categorisation, they won't be in the grey figure (correct per your rule).
If you DO want them counted, mark their codes as Materials/Labour in Admin ->
Account Categorisation.
