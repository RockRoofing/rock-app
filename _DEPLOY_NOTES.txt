DEPLOY NOTES - Gross Margin trend now = rolling trailing-12-month line
======================================================================

CONTEXT (what we learned)
-------------------------
The wild monthly swings (Jun 6.5%, May -71%, Apr 48%) were NOT a bug. They are
accrual-timing effects: your WIP journals (code 631, balance-sheet) post against
code 200 Sales each month-end and auto-reverse the next month, so any single month's
Sales is inflated or depressed by the WIP accrual. Costs and the matching sale also
fall in different months. Over a full year this nets out - blended 6 months already
came to 25.6%, right in the expected 22-27% band.

Decision: keep WIP as revenue (no code change needed for that - it already flows
through code 200 on the P&L and nets out over 12 months), and make the TREND show
the trailing-12 rolling margin so the line reflects the trustworthy figure instead
of the monthly noise.

WHAT CHANGED
------------
  - Headline: unchanged - trailing 12-month Gross Margin (rolling annual).
  - Trend line: now 6 points, each a FULL trailing-12-month margin ending that
    month (was individual monthly margins). The newest trend point equals the
    headline by construction. No more -71% spikes.
  - To build 6 rolling-12 points the endpoint now fetches 17 completed months
    (12 + 6 - 1). Each month is fetched ONCE and the rolling windows are computed
    in memory - no duplicate calls.
  - "Avg/mo" line removed from this card (an average of rolling averages would
    mislead; the headline is already the figure that matters).
  - The drill still lists the raw monthly detail plus the trailing-12 total and the
    per-code diagnostic.

SPEED NOTE
----------
This now pulls 17 monthly P&L reports from Xero per Scorecard load (~4-5 seconds,
more API calls). The older months don't change, so if this feels slow we should
cache them in Redis and only re-fetch the most recent month on each load. Say the
word and I'll add that - it would cut it to ~1 call most of the time.
